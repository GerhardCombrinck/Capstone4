Route Types

Climbing is all about getting to the top on your own steam, but there are a few diffenet methods of doing it.

Bouldering

Bouldering involves climbing without a rope but using a matress for safety. Bouldering ruites are usually shorter, but very intense. Bouldering can be done indoors and outdoors.

Sport Climbing

Sport Climbing involves climbing longer routes with a rope which is used to pretect the climber during falls. Climbing achors (hangers) are bolted into the wall/rock. There are two styles of sport climbing, namely Top-Rope climbing and Lead climbing. Top-Rope is when the rope is already on the route and the rope is pulled up as the climber climbs. Lead climbing is when the rope is not on the route when climbing begins, the climber progressively secures the rope to anchor points which are spaced along the route as he/she goes.

Trad Climbing

Trad Climbing is exactly like lead stye sport climbing but there are no permanent bolts or fixtures to secure to, the climber uses various wedges and cams that are placed in a strategic manner to secure him/her self in the event of a fall. A collection of trad gear is normally called a trad rack.

Deep Water Solo

This is quite unique and refers to when the route being climbed is directly above a deep body of water. In this case the climber uses no safety devices as he/she can simply fall into the water.

Free Solo

This is just crazy, but super cool. You climb with only shoes and chalk, no rope, no mats, no water. A single mistake and it will be your last. There are not may climbers in this category but these are without question the elite of the sport.
